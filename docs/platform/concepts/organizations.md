# Organizations & workspaces

Backfield is organized in a simple hierarchy. Understanding it helps you know where settings live and who can see what.

## Organization

An **organization** is your newsroom or company — the top-level account. It owns everything else: the people who have access, your shared settings (AI models, integrations), your Stylebook catalogs, and all of your projects.

Most teams have a single organization.

## Workspace

A **workspace** is an optional way to group related projects under an organization — for example, by desk, beat, or initiative. Workspaces are mainly an organizational convenience; if your team is small, you may not need more than the default.

A workspace also sets some shared defaults for the projects inside it, such as which [Stylebook](../stylebook/stylebooks.md) those projects use unless a project chooses a different one.

## Project

A **project** is where the day-to-day work happens — the flows you build, the runs you execute, and the catalog of people, places, and organizations that work produces. Almost everything you do in Backfield happens inside a project.

Projects are covered in detail in [Projects](projects.md).

## How this affects access and settings

- **People** are invited to the **organization**, then given access to projects. See [Users & access](users.md).
- **Shared settings** like [AI models](../settings/ai-models.md) and [integrations](../settings/integrations.md) are defined once at the organization level, and individual projects can override them where it makes sense.
- **Stylebook catalogs** belong to the organization and can be shared across projects.
